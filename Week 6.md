## Content Insights
- The team should never wait for others to complete their tasks before starting their own work. This sequential dependency relationship can cause bottlenecks and reduce efficiency. Instead, whenever possible, work should be carried out in parallel, and team members should coordinate the dependency relationships rather than waiting for them to be completed. This requires breaking the work into smaller, more independent parts so that it can be advanced simultaneously.
- The role of the project task board goes far beyond tracking progress. They externalize the team's memory and thinking patterns. When tasks are visible to everyone, there is no need to rely on memory to understand what is happening. This prevents tasks from being forgotten or progress from stagnating unnoticed. The visual nature of the task board can also quickly reveal where the bottlenecks are - when a certain column has too many tasks, the team can see the choke points and respond.
- Effective version update messages (commit messages or project updates) are not just documents, but also communication tools. A well-written update content can clearly tell team members and reviewers what work has been completed, without them having to browse the code or raise questions. This asynchronous communication method saves time for everyone and enables team coordination even if team members work at different times.
- If the daily stand-up meeting is conducted properly, it can achieve three purposes: reminding team members of the direction they are working towards, early detection of obstacles, and establishing responsibility through public commitments. The key is to be brief and focused.
